#  Complex Networks

This repository contains materials and implementations related to the study of **complex networks**, focusing on their representation, analysis, modeling, and real-world applications.

---

## Course Overview
The course introduces the fundamental concepts of **complex network theory**, providing the essential tools for the representation, modeling, characterization, and classification of networks.

These concepts are applied to the analysis of real systems such as:
- The Internet and World Wide Web  
- Social networks  
- Language and communication systems  
- Biological systems (e.g., cellular interactions, brain networks)  
- Food webs  
- Transportation and road networks  

---

### - Network Representation
- Graph-based representation of complex systems  
- Weighted and spatial networks  

### - Topological Characterization
- Degree and degree distribution  
- Clustering coefficient  
- Shortest path length  
- Cycles and motifs  
- Centrality measures  
- Spectral, hierarchical, and fractal measures  
- Community structure  

### - Network Models
- Random graphs  
- Small-world networks  
- Scale-free networks  
- Hierarchical models  
- Configuration model  

### - Community Detection
- Methods for identifying communities and modular structures  

### - Dynamical Processes on Networks
- Random walks  
- Failures and attacks  
- Cascade failures  
- Communication and congestion  
- Epidemic spreading  
- Opinion dynamics  
- Synchronization and collective behavior  

### - Optimization and Navigation
- Flow optimization  
- Network robustness and resilience  
- Search and navigation in networks  
- Generating functions  

### - Algorithms
- PageRank  
- Betweenness centrality  
- Community detection algorithms  
- Synchronization methods  
- Cascade failure models  
- Random walk algorithms  

### - Applications
- Social networks  
- Internet and World Wide Web  
- Bioinformatics and brain networks  
- Transportation systems  
- Language processing  
- Image processing and pattern recognition

## 🎯 Objective

The goal of this repository is to explore the theoretical foundations and computational approaches of complex networks, highlighting their applications in diverse real-world systems.
